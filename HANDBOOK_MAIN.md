## 1. What is 30DLC? — The Origin Story

**30 Day Listing Challenge (30DLC)** is a competitive 30-day real estate program powered by Fello.ai. Teams and individual agents compete to generate the most listings through daily prospecting, webinar attendance, AI coaching, and social engagement.

### How It Started

Three years ago, Ryan Young (Founder & CEO of Fello, Owner of The Young Team) posted a bold public challenge: *set 30 listing appointments in 30 days — strictly from the database. No inbound leads. No live transfers. Just contacts agents had written off.*

> *"The work you do in the next 30 days is building your May, June, and July. The money shows up 90 days later."*
> — Ryan Young

It started as a personal public commitment. Once it was live, people cheered him on, and there was no going back. Every day he documented calls, wins, frustrations — live. It changed his team's P&L and sparked a national movement.

**The results:**
- 2025: The Young Team set **125 listing appointments in March** → had their most profitable June, July, and August ever
- 2024: 40 listing appointments from one team → doubled production
- Past participants have reported maintaining 30+ active listings at a time, 38% year-over-year business growth, and fully transformed prospecting habits

### 2026 Theme: Tactics Over Theory

In 2025, 30DLC brought in inspirational industry titans (Gary Keller, Tom Ferry, etc.) for motivation. In 2026, the focus shifts to **tactical operators** — team leaders and agents who are actually in the trenches, setting appointments daily, and opening their playbooks on how they do it.

**Real operators. Real playbooks. Real results — not just theory.**

### Why It Works

- Public commitment creates accountability you can't buy
- Community competition creates energy you can't manufacture
- 30 days of focus compounds into 90+ days of revenue
- Gamified habits change your business permanently — not just for the challenge

### Team Leader Challenge — $25,000

A **separate competition** exclusively for team leaders. The $25,000 winner must spend it on their team (trip, night out, shopping spree, celebration event — not kept personally).

- Leaderboard is **blind** — you can see your rank but not actual point gaps
- Top 5 team leaders qualify (not top 3)
- **Winner takes all** — no drawing; highest appointments + agreements signed wins
- The Young Team will audit entries (CRM checks, listing verification)
- Must have a team to enter — prize must be spent on team members

### How Winners Are Selected (Team Categories)

1. Top 3 teams in each category advance
2. A digital wheel draw selects: 3rd place (first drawn), 2nd place (second drawn), 1st place (last remaining)
3. Every team in the top 3 has an equal 1/3 chance of winning first place
4. Point margin doesn't matter — being in the top 3 is all that counts

## 4. Complete Scoring System

**Total Score = Daily Tracking + Calls + Appointments + Agreements + Social Posts + Webinar Attendance**

### Pre-Challenge Bonus Points (Earn Before Day 1)

| Activity | Points | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Set up your 30DC portal | 50 pts | One-time, before challenge starts |
| Post "I'm In" social video with #Fello30DC | 50 pts | One-time, public post with hashtag |
| Team leader category social post with #Fello30DC | 50 pts | Team leaders only |

### Daily Activity Points

| Activity | Points | What Counts |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| **Daily Tracking** | 25 pts | Log your entry within 48 hours of activity — you earn this just for logging |
| **2+ Minute Conversation** (Calls Made) | 2 pts each | Any completed prospecting call lasting 2+ minutes |
| **Listing Appointment Set** | 50 pts each | Confirmed listing appointment scheduled |
| **Listing Agreement Signed** | 100 pts each | Signed listing agreement (also called "Listings Taken") |
| **Daily Social Post** | 15 pts each | Public real estate post with #Fello30DC |

### Team Leader Challenge Points (separate scoring)

| Activity | Points |
|----------|--------|
| Listing Appointment Set (by leader) | 50 pts |
| Listing Agreement Signed (by team member who attended) | 100 pts |

> **Team Leader Note**: As team leader, you do not have to go on the appointment or be the agent of record. You set the appointment from your database — your agent attends and signs the agreement. The points count for both the team competition AND the team leader challenge.

### Full Day Scoring Example

```
Daily tracking (logged entry)          = 25 pts
8 calls (2+ minutes each) × 2 pts     = 16 pts
2 appointments set × 50 pts           = 100 pts
1 agreement signed × 100 pts          = 100 pts
1 social post × 15 pts                = 15 pts
─────────────────────────────────────────────
Total today                            = 256 pts
```

### Score Composition on Leaderboard

The leaderboard tracks four separate columns:
```
Rank | Team | Entries | Webinar | Social | Total
```

- **Entries** = daily tracking + calls + appointments + agreements
- **Webinar** = attendance at webinars through the portal
- **Social** = logged social posts
- **Total** = sum of all three

## 7. Pre-Challenge Preparation — 10-Step Foundation

Complete these before Week 1 starts to maximize every single day of the challenge.

> *"The pre-pool pump — before you go to the pool, you might wanna hit a couple push-ups."* — Ryan Young

### 10-Step Challenge Foundation

| Step | Action | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **1. Set Goals** | Team + Individual | Each agent commits to their number. Roll up to team goal. Stretch 10–20% above the sum. |
| **2. Plan Prizes + Celebration** | Team prize + weekly individual rewards | Lock in the end-of-challenge celebration NOW — trip, dinner, night out |
| **3. Call Your Shot Publicly** | Everyone posts an "I'm In" video | Balloons, whiteboard, film it together. Post with #Fello30DC. |
| **4. Scoreboard Alignment** | Physical scoreboard + portal | Update daily at team huddle. Makes progress tangible. |
| **5. Accountability Structure** | Buddy pairs or pods of 3–4 agents | Group text. Tuesday call nights. Daily check-ins. |
| **6. Ops Participation** | Get your operations team involved | Golden Letters, referrals, database cleaning, past client outreach |
| **7. Drive Daily Social Momentum** | Commit to daily posts (wins AND losses) | Your community = free accountability engine |
| **8. Calendar Build-Out** | Build March execution calendar | Block webinars, call nights, lead gen blocks NOW |
| **9. Database Strategy** | Enrich your Fello database (free) | Work with your CSM to identify all homeowners, build high-intent segments |
| **10. Fello Pre-Pool Pump** | Schedule time with your CSM | Build segments, prioritize high-intent leads, distribute unassigned opps |

### Database Prep with Fello (Free — Do Before Day 1)

These steps with your Fello Customer Success Manager cost nothing and dramatically increase your call list quality:

1. **Database Enrichment** — Fello enriches your entire contact database to identify all homeowners, their equity, and how long they've been in their home. Ryan: *"You will see it completely changes the way you look at your database."*
2. **High-Intent Segments** — Build segments of contacts showing home sale signals. Call these first.
3. **Cash Offer Campaign** — Set up a free cash offer email campaign to prime your database. High-intent responders are your warmest leads.
4. **Distribute Unassigned Leads** — Move ALL pond/unassigned Fello leads to named agents before Day 1. *"If there is no owner, there is no ownership."* — Ryan Young
5. **Expired in Database** — Fello flags contacts who listed with another agent and failed to sell. These are high-motivation leads already in your database.
6. **Prepare Buyer Needs List** — List all active buyers and what they're looking for. Buyers are bait to find sellers.

## 12. Goal-Setting Framework

### Why Goals Must Be Bottom-Up, Not Top-Down

The most common mistake: team leaders announce a round-number team goal without getting agent buy-in first. This creates a number with no ownership.

**The Right Approach (Ryan Young's method):**
1. Sit with each agent individually
2. Ask: *"Looking at your pipeline and your schedule for the next 30 days, how many listing appointments can you commit to setting?"*
3. Record their commitment — make it their number, not yours
4. Roll all individual numbers up to get the real team goal
5. As leader, stretch that sum by 10–20% for the official team target

> *"I'm gonna hold you accountable to that. Jamie, six. Steven, how many can you do? Three? Awesome."* — Ryan Young

### Step 1 — Establish Your Honest Baseline

| Activity | Your Typical Number |
|----------|-------------------|
| Calls per day | _____ |
| Appointments per week | _____ |
| Agreements per month | _____ |

### Step 2 — Apply the Stretch Formula

**Target = Baseline × 1.3 to 1.5** (30–50% above your normal pace)

Going for 2× causes burnout. Going for 1× means no growth. The 30–50% stretch is achievable with focused daily habits.

| Baseline | Conservative (×1.3) | Aggressive (×1.5) |
|----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| 5 calls/day | 6–7/day | 7–8/day |
| 8 calls/day | 10–11/day | 12/day |
| 10 calls/day | 13/day | 15/day |
| 1 appt/week | 1–2/week | 2/week |

### Step 3 — Set Your 30-Day Total Goal

```
Daily goal × working days = 30-day target
```

**Example (10 calls/day, 20 working days):**
```
Calls: 10 × 20 = 200 calls total
Appointments: 2/week × 4 weeks = 8 appointments
Agreements: 1–2 over the challenge
```

### Step 4 — Work Backwards from a Target Score

```
Target: 4,000 pts over 28 days
Daily needed: 4,000 ÷ 28 ≈ 143 pts/day
+ Daily tracking bonus: 25 pts
Remaining needed: 118 pts from activities

Option A: 5 calls (10 pts) + 2 appts (100 pts) + 1 social (15 pts) = 125 pts
Option B: 10 calls (20 pts) + 1 appt (50 pts) + 1 agreement (100 pts) = 170 pts
```

Ask your AI Coach: *"Based on my current score and the number of days left, what daily targets do I need to finish in the top 3 of my category?"*

### Step 5 — Weekly Milestone Goals

| Week | Days | Focus | Mindset |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| Week 1 | 1–7 | Build the habit | Baseline +20%. Momentum over perfection. |
| Week 2 | 8–14 | Push harder | Baseline +30%. Start seeing appointments stack up. |
| Week 3 | 15–21 | Sprint | Baseline +50%. This is where most teams fade — don't. |
| Week 4 | 22–28 | Finish strong | Maximum effort. You're planting and harvesting simultaneously. |

### Public Commitment — Non-Negotiable

Every agent on the team should publicly post their individual goal. Not just the team goal — each person's personal number.

> *"I'm Ryan with the Young Team. Over the next 30 days I'm going to set 10 listing appointments. The most I've ever set in 30 days is three. So I'm a little scared. But I would love your support."*

This creates personal accountability to your entire network — family, friends, past clients, sphere — who will encourage you when you feel like quitting in Week 2.

### Scheduling Calls with Part-Time / Dual-Career Agents

**Situation**: Agents who have day jobs or are unavailable during certain hours (e.g., 9–5, or 3:30–5:30 PM).

**Best prospecting windows by availability:**

| Agent Free Window | Best Call Times | Why |
|-------------------|-----------------|-----|
| Before 9 AM | 7:00–8:30 AM | Homeowners haven't started their day; early callers are memorable |
| Lunch | 12:00–1:00 PM | People away from desks, more likely to answer personal calls |
| After 5:30 PM | 5:30–7:30 PM | Homeowners home, most receptive for residential calls |
| Weekends | Sat 9 AM–12 PM | Single highest answer rate for residential prospecting |

**Sample schedule for agent free after 5:30 PM:**
```
7:00–8:30 AM    Morning call block: 8–12 calls
12:00–12:30 PM  Lunch blitz: 5 targeted calls
6:00–7:30 PM    Evening calls: 10–15 calls
Sat 9 AM–12 PM  Weekend sprint: 15–20 calls
─────────────────────────────────────────
Daily: ~25 calls → 50 pts from calls alone
```

**Key principle**: The challenge does not require business hours. Prospecting calls at 7 AM or 7 PM count exactly the same. List presentations can be booked evenings or weekends when agents are fully available.

**Morning call opener:**

### Building Your Database from Scratch

**Situation**: New market, small database, or fewer than 100 contacts.

**Goal**: Add 5 new contacts every day → 150 new contacts by Day 30.

**8 strategies:**

1. **Circle Prospecting** — For every listing (yours or nearby in MLS), call the 50 nearest homeowners. *"I'm working with buyers looking in your neighborhood. Have you ever thought about selling?"* = 1 listing → 50 new contacts

2. **Expired Listings** — Pull daily from MLS. These owners wanted to sell and didn't. Call same day they expire (before other agents). 2–10 expireds/day in most markets.

3. **FSBOs** — Pull from Zillow, FSBO.com, Facebook Marketplace. 80% eventually list with an agent. Follow up weekly for 4–6 weeks.

4. **Sphere Expansion** — Text everyone in your phone: *"I'm doing a 30-day real estate challenge. Do you know anyone thinking of buying or selling? I'd love the intro."* 100 contacts × 10% = 10 warm leads.

5. **Open Houses** — Host every weekend. Collect sign-in: name, email, phone, timeframe. 1 open house = 5–20 new contacts.

6. **Social Lead Generation** — Post daily. CTA: *"Comment 'HOME' and I'll send you a free home value report."* Add all responders.

7. **Past Client Referral Calls** — *"I'm in a 30-day challenge. Do you know anyone thinking of moving in the next year?"* Warmest source.

8. **Geographic Farming** — Pick 200–500 homes. Mail + call over 30 days. 5% response = 10–25 new leads.

## 15. Week-by-Week Battle Plan

### Week 1 (Days 1–7): Launch Hot, Build the Habit

### Week 2 (Days 8–14): Push Harder

**Focus**: Appointments. Keep dialing. Don't stop when you start getting appointments.

- You'll start going on actual appointments — keep prospecting in parallel
- Use your buyer needs to reverse prospect sellers (most powerful script)
- Circle prospect around any new listings
- Attack expireds in Fello — those who listed with competitors and didn't sell
- Keep posting daily social updates — your network is following your story now
- Call nights: show up even when tired

**Key mindset**: *"The biggest mistake in Week 2 is thinking 'I got some appointments, I'm good.' You're NOT good until you keep the pipeline full."*

### Week 3 (Days 15–21): Sprint Through the Wall

**Focus**: Discipline. This is where the leaderboard separates.

- You're simultaneously prospecting, going on appointments, taking listings to market
- PROTECT morning lead gen — everything else moves around it
- Ops team helps manage listings going active so you stay on phones
- Hold a team re-energizing session mid-week

**Key mindset**: *"Week 3 is where most people fall off. If you push through, you separate. If you quit, you hand it to someone else."*

### Week 4 (Days 22–28): Finish Line Sprint

**Focus**: Maximum effort. Leave nothing on the table.

- Every day counts — you can still shift leaderboard position significantly

- Celebrate milestones — every agreement signed is a big moment
- AI Coach: *"It's the final week. I'm X points back from 3rd place. What do I need to do every day to catch up?"*

**Key mindset**: *"You don't know if you're 1 point back or 1,000 points back from the next team. You might be on the edge. Go as if you are."*
